To support you in making a well-informed decision, we would like to offer a few practical considerations when reviewing certification providers:

1. Points to consider when selecting a certification body
 * Overall cost of services. We recommend comparing proposals carefully and confirming what is included. Please ensure that quotations cover all potential costs, such as auditor travel and accommodation, registration fees, certificate issuance, surveillance activities, and any reviews of corrective actions.
 * Reputation and experience. It may be helpful to request client references, case studies, or examples of experience within your sector.
 * Responsiveness and service quality. Clear communication, professionalism, and timely responses are often good indicators of how smoothly the certification process will proceed.

2. Points to verify once you have selected a certification body
 * Accreditation credentials. Please request a copy of the accreditation certificate and scope, and confirm that they cover the relevant standard and industry activities for your organisation.
 * Contractual details. Ensure that the agreement is concluded with the same legal entity named on the accreditation certificate. The contract should also state that accreditation will be maintained throughout the engagement and that you will be notified promptly of any changes.
 * Certificate validity and public listing. Once issued, the certificate should display the certification body’s accreditation number. You may also wish to confirm that the certificate can be publicly verified both on the certification body’s website and within the IAF CertSearch database operated by the International Accreditation Forum.
